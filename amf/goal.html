Impact of Malaria (data and embed sourced from
OurWorldInData.org):

The WHO has provided global estimates of the number of people who die from malaria since the start of the twenty-first century. From 896,000 deaths in 2000 to 562,000 deaths in 2015, the worldwide death toll decreased by over 40% between those years. Progress has since slowed. Because of the disruptions brought on by the Coronavirus pandemic, mortality actually rose in 2020. 2 Malaria affects Africa more than any other part of the world; in 2019, 96% of all malaria-related fatalities worldwide happened there. Yet, Africa has also made the most advancements globally: the number of deaths has drastically decreased since 2000.
